Design System

The reference for a premium, intentional interface — light canvas, deep navy ink, one electric-blue accent, amber as a rare spark, and the colourful original logo as the single pop of colour. Every rule here exists to avoid the generic “vibe-coded” look.

Principles

Premium, not vibe-coded

AI generators predict the median and apply every mechanism at once — bordered + shadowed + rounded cards with a pill eyebrow on top, a purple gradient, Inter, and fade-in-everything. Premium design is the deliberate refusal of the median: pick one mechanism per job, define a finite token scale, and commit.

The 10 fastest tells of a vibe-coded site

  1. Purple/indigo gradient on white as the hero.
  2. Inter / system / Space Grotesk as the brand face.
  3. An eyebrow or badge pill above every heading.
  4. Uniform 3-column cards with rounded-square icon tiles.
  5. Section padding equal to card padding.
  6. Border + diffuse black shadow on the same resting card.
  7. Pure greys (#808080 / #000 / #fff), zero saturation.
  8. One global radius, everything over-rounded.
  9. Fade-in-on-scroll on everything, 500ms+, default easing.
  10. No focal point — every element equal weight.

Foundations

Typography

One family — Plus Jakarta Sans — carrying display character through weight (800) and tight negative tracking, not a second face. Body capped at a 66ch measure. Fluid sizes via clamp() between a 320px and 1280px viewport (Utopia method).

Do
  • Bigger heading → tighter tracking (−0.02 to −0.03em).
  • Labels get positive tracking (+0.08em) — they read as metadata.
  • ≤3 weights; weight contrast at the extremes (400 / 800).
  • Body measure ≤ 66ch.
Don't
  • Ship Inter / Roboto / system as the brand face.
  • Pair adjacent weights (400 vs 600) for hierarchy.
  • Leave big headings at default (loose) tracking.
  • Full-bleed body lines.
Verdict

Do we need eyebrows above every heading? No.

The eyebrow isn't slop — its reflexive repetition is. A pill/badge eyebrow stamped above every section (and above the hero H1) is a top tell. We keep an eyebrow only when all hold: (1) it categorises rather than decorates, (2) the headline is long or keyword-poor, (3) it's used on a few sections, never all, and (4) something else is the section's focal point. Styled as subordinate metadata — 13px, weight 600, uppercase, positive tracking, muted — never a coloured pill.

In this build: heroes and product/section pages may carry one categorising eyebrow; the homepage content sections drop them and let the headline carry the section.

Hero height — never fixed 100vh

On mobile, 100vh is taller than the visible area (address bar) and clips content. Use min-height: 100svh with content-driven growth and fluid padding-block; cap on large screens. Inner heroes here are type-forward with generous top padding to clear the fixed nav, not full-height.

No global rounded-everything. Nested: inner = outer − padding.

Elevation — flat by default

Resting cards have no border + no shadow — group with tone and whitespace, or a hairline. Shadows exist only for floating overlays (menus, dialogs), hue-tinted and layered, never flat black.
